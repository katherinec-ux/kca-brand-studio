The Real Role of a Brand Strategist
(And Why It's Misunderstood)

Katherine Chalhoub · 10 April 2026 · 3 min read

What does a brand strategist actually do?

A brand strategist defines positioning, direction, and messaging. Not just visuals. Strategy sits underneath all of the creative — it shapes how a brand operates in market.

Why this role is misunderstood

Because brand is often associated with design, creative, and aesthetics. But strategy sits underneath all of it. Design is the execution of strategic decisions — not the strategy itself.

"Brand strategy is structural, not aesthetic. It defines direction and decision-making — and directly impacts commercial outcomes."

Why this matters commercially

Brand strategy impacts marketing performance, conversion, and growth. It defines how a brand operates in market — what it says, who it speaks to, and why it is different. Without it, every downstream decision — creative, copy, media — lacks a clear foundation.

Key takeaways

  • Brand strategy is structural, not aesthetic
  • It defines direction and decision-making
  • It directly impacts commercial outcomes

If you're investing in brand, make sure it's strategic.
