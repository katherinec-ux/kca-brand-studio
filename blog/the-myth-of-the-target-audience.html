The Myth of the "Target Audience"
(And What to Define Instead)

Katherine Chalhoub · 10 April 2026 · 3 min read

Why are traditional target audiences ineffective?

Because demographics do not explain behaviour. Knowing age, gender, and location does not explain why someone chooses a brand, what they value, or what drives action.

"Demographics are not enough for brand strategy. Behaviour and mindset drive decisions."

What should brands define instead?

1. Context

When and why the brand matters.

2. Mindset

What the audience believes and prioritises.

3. Tension

The problem they are trying to solve.

Why this improves performance

When you define audience this way, messaging becomes more relevant, positioning becomes clearer, and conversion improves. Because you're addressing real decision drivers — not surface-level characteristics.

Key takeaways

  • Demographics are not enough for brand strategy
  • Behaviour and mindset drive decisions
  • Context defines relevance
  • Strong brands align to real-world situations

If your messaging isn't landing, your audience definition may be too broad.
