Why Most Brand Guidelines
Don't Get Used

Katherine Chalhoub · 10 April 2026 · 3 min read

Why are brand guidelines often ignored?

Because they are not practical. Most guidelines are too complex, too theoretical, and not actionable. They sit in a folder and never make it into daily use.

"Usability is more important than completeness. Strong brands are consistently applied."

What makes brand guidelines effective?

They should be clear, usable, and easy to apply. Not a document full of rules — a resource that actually supports execution.

What should strong guidelines include?

  • Messaging examples
  • Tone of voice application
  • Real-world use cases

Not just rules. Examples. Context. Practical guidance that teams can pick up and use immediately.

Key takeaways

  • Usability is more important than completeness
  • Guidelines should support execution
  • Strong brands are consistently applied

If your brand isn't being used correctly, it's not clear enough.
