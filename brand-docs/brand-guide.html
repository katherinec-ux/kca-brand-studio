01 — Brand Foundation

Who we are

KCA Brand Studio is a strategy-led brand practice for founders and emerging businesses. We don't start with aesthetics. We start with positioning — then build a brand that's impossible to ignore and built to convert.

Mission
Make every brand impossible to ignore.
We exist to give founders the clarity, strategy, and identity they need to stand apart and drive real commercial outcomes.
Positioning
Strategy-led. Conversion-aware. Built to perform.
Not a design studio. Not a freelancer. A strategic practice that starts with positioning and ends with a brand ready to launch.
Belief
Every brand deserves a strategy as strong as its ambition.
Beautiful without strategy is just decoration. We build brands that resonate, stand out, and convert.

Brand Story

The name behind the studio

KCA stands for Katherine Chalhoub Arcuri — but the abbreviated form carries deliberate weight. Inspired by the late-career reinvention of Kid A (Radiohead's landmark departure from the expected), the name signals the same spirit: experimentation, authorship, and a departure from the safe and predictable.

The "K" is a marker of authorship. This brand carries Kath's name because every project carries her thinking, her direction, and her commercial intent — personally.

"If your brand can't articulate why it's different — it doesn't matter how beautiful it is. It won't convert."

— Katherine Chalhoub Arcuri, Founder

Core Taglines

Primary Tagline
"Built to stand out. Designed to perform."
Used across website footer, signatures, capability statements. Leads with differentiation, closes with commercial intent.
Hero Headline
"Your brand shouldn't blend in. Let's make it impossible to ignore."
Primary hero statement. Splits across two beats — problem / solution. The italic emphasis carries the brand's energy.
Alternative Tagline
"Defined differently. Built properly."
Use in secondary placements, social captions, presentations. Reinforces the anti-backwards-branding philosophy.

03 — Voice & Tone

How we speak

KCA Brand Studio's voice is the same whether writing a proposal, a social caption, or a website headline. It's direct, commercially grounded, and carries the confidence of someone who's been in the room.

Attribute
Direct
Lead with the point. No softening. No throat-clearing. Say the thing, then move on.
Attribute
Confident
No hedging. No "I think" or "perhaps". Positions are stated as positions.
Attribute
Commercial
Every message ties back to outcomes: resonate, stand out, convert. Business-first thinking.
Attribute
Human
Founder voice — personal, warm, no jargon. Sounds like Kath, not a corporate handbook.

Language We Use

Cut through Defined Positioned Built properly Strategy-led Conversion-aware Commercial intent Stand apart Clarity Uncover Immersion Foundation No fluff High-touch

Language We Avoid

Creative solutions Passionate about Unique Bespoke Synergy Holistic Leverage World-class Seamless Full-service End-to-end solutions

Copy Examples — Good vs. Avoid

Write like this
"No drawn-out timelines. No endless revisions. A concentrated process designed to get you to market with clarity and confidence."
"Positioning first. Aesthetics second. We figure out where you sit in the market before we talk colours and fonts."
"A process that uncovers — not assumes."
"Ready to get clear on positioning and stand out in market? No fluff. No sales pitch. Just a straight conversation about your brand."
Avoid this
"We offer bespoke, holistic creative solutions tailored to your unique needs."
"Our passionate team of world-class designers will leverage your brand to deliver seamless synergies."
"We'd love to explore working with you on your exciting project!"
"We believe in the power of storytelling to connect with audiences in a meaningful way."

Writing Principles

Principle 01
Lead with the problem
Start with what the reader is experiencing. "Most brands are built backwards." earns attention before the solution is offered.
Principle 02
Short sentences win
One idea per sentence. Paragraphs rarely exceed three sentences. White space is generous. Rhythm matters.
Principle 03
Name the outcome
Every service, every section, every CTA ends with a commercial outcome. Resonate. Stand out. Convert. Don't leave it implicit.

07 — Imagery & Photography

Visual direction

Photography and imagery reinforce the brand's premium, personal, and strategy-grounded identity. The visual language should feel like a premium consultancy — not a generic marketing agency. Every image selected should feel intentional, editorial, and human.

Hero Imagery
Analog objects — typewriters, notebooks, craft tools. Shot in warm, natural light with shallow depth of field. Muted, editorial tone. Suggests craft, process, and intentionality rather than digital polish.
Founder Portrait
Circular crop with decorative ring overlays. Warm, natural light — window-lit or soft outdoor. No studio flash. The ring treatment adds editorial separation and signals premium personal branding.
Brand Context
Strategy sessions, brand materials in context, founder at work. Must feel real and candid — not staged. Props that signal serious work: mood boards, print-outs, coffee, notebooks. Never a suits-and-handshake aesthetic.

Photography Selection — Approved vs. Avoid

Approved Photography
  • Warm, natural light — window-lit or soft outdoor settings
  • Analog props: typewriters, printed type specimens, notebooks, letterpress
  • Founder in candid working contexts — not staged or posed corporate
  • Shallow depth of field — subject sharp, background soft
  • Slightly muted, filmic tone — editorial, not Instagram-filtered
  • Close-up details of craft: hands on keyboard, pen on paper, type specimen
  • Warm-toned interiors that complement the cream/forest palette
Photography to Avoid
  • Generic stock photography — suits, handshakes, fake boardroom smiles
  • Highly saturated or heavy Instagram-style filters
  • White studio shots with grey gradient backgrounds
  • Abstract digital or tech-themed imagery (code, data, circuits)
  • Flat lay product shots that feel commercial rather than editorial
  • Any image that places focus on tools over thinking and strategy
  • Low-resolution, over-compressed, or watermarked files

08 — Iconography

Icon system

KCA Brand Studio uses a consistent SVG line icon system across the site — process steps, differentiator cards, and feature highlights. All icons follow a single, precise style rule: 1.5px stroke weight with rounded terminals. Never filled, never heavy.

Icon rule: All icons are SVG line icons. No filled/solid icons. No outline-with-fill combinations. stroke-width: 1.5, stroke-linecap: round, stroke-linejoin: round. Icons are always Forest Green (#1E3D28) on a Light Sage container, reversing to White on Forest Green on hover.

09 — Partner Logo Treatment

Client & partner logos

Partner and client brand logos appear across the KCA website in a consistent, restrained treatment. All logos are rendered in grayscale on a warm cream tile — colour restores on hover. This approach maintains KCA's visual authority while crediting the brands worked with.

Grayscale rule: All partner logos are displayed as filter: grayscale(100%); opacity: 0.8; mix-blend-mode: multiply at rest. On hover, filter is removed and opacity returns to 1. This ensures no competitor or client brand visually competes with KCA's own palette.

12 — Do's & Don'ts

Brand guardrails

These rules protect the brand's premium positioning and strategic clarity. They apply across all channels, formats, and contexts.

Brand Do's
  • Always lead with strategy — positioning before aesthetics in all written and visual communication
  • Use the three-colour primary palette (Green, Cream, Terracotta) as the primary system
  • Reserve italic Cormorant Garamond for emotional accent moments — headlines, quotes, pull statements
  • Keep button copy in UPPERCASE DM Sans — never mixed case or sentence case
  • Use Olive (#8A8C64) for labels, eyebrows, and secondary data — never as a headline colour
  • Use the Terracotta for CTAs and the single accent band — not liberally throughout layouts
  • Maintain generous white space — the brand breathes
  • Use the exact hex values specified — do not eyeball approximations
  • Keep copy short. Headlines under 12 words. Body copy under 3 sentences per paragraph.
  • Attribute all copy with commercial intent — end every message with an outcome
Brand Don'ts
  • Don't use Terracotta as a background for long-form content sections
  • Don't mix serif and sans-serif in the same button
  • Don't add drop shadows to logos, type, or UI elements beyond the defined elevation system
  • Don't use any typeface outside Cormorant Garamond and DM Sans (except Jost for Direction 01/Couture logo only)
  • Don't present the brand as a "design studio" — always frame as strategy-led and commercial
  • Don't use the word "passion", "bespoke", "holistic", or "unique" in any copy
  • Don't apply agency-speak — the voice is founder-to-founder, direct and human
  • Don't crowd layouts — each section has a single job. One message. One action.
  • Don't scale the Terracotta accent heavily — one CTA band per page, one badge per card
  • Don't deviate from the defined type scale — custom font sizes break visual rhythm
